What is this

An Ideathon. Not a Hackathon.

You don't need to code. You need to think. Design an AI-powered solution to a real-world challenge — pitch it to industry experts. Open to everyone, no experience required.

◈
Open to Everyone
First year or final year. Technical or non-technical. Zero experience is fine. Ideas and human thinking win here — not lines of code.
◉
Hidden Challenge
The theme is sealed until April 25th. Your team has the full day to define the problem, design an AI solution, and pitch it to a jury of active CEOs.
⬡
Expert Support
A joint workshop by two industry CEOs opens the day. The IEEE committee guides your team throughout — you're never alone in this.
🖥️
No personal laptop needed on April 25th. All project presentations will be displayed using the organization's equipment on the day. Once your project is submitted before April 24 at 11:59 PM, no modifications are allowed — what you submit is what you pitch.

The Full Journey

Three Dates. One Mission.

The ideathon doesn't start on April 25th — it starts on April 22nd. Here's the complete roadmap.

April 22
Pre-Event · Workshop Day
1
🔓 Challenge Theme Reveal
The moment arrives. The challenge theme is announced live for the first time — the hidden topic you've been curious about since you registered. This is what the entire ideathon is built around. You'll have the full weekend to build your project around it.
2
Workshop — Design Thinking for Impact
Facilitated by a special guest speaker ✦ Identity revealed on the day
A hands-on session walking you through the ideation canvas — Problem → AI Role → Impact — using a neutral real-world example. You'll practice the exact framework you'll use to build your project over the weekend, so you arrive on April 25th already trained.

April 24
Submission Deadline
Project Submission
Deadline: April 24 · 11:59 PM — No exceptions
You have April 22nd's session and the full weekend to build your solution. Submit your completed project — idea, canvas, and presentation — before midnight. On April 25th you arrive to pitch it. No modifications are accepted after submission. What you submit is what gets presented to the jury using the organization's equipment.
April 25
Ideathon Day · 8:00 AM – 4:00 PM
01
Registration
Teams check in and receive final event materials. Your project is already submitted — today is about the pitch.
08:00 – 09:00
02
Opening Ceremony & Guest Talk
Official opening of the ideathon. A special guest — a CEO active in AI and technology — takes the stage for the opening keynote. Their identity is revealed live on the day.
09:00 – 10:00
03
Pitching Workshop
A dedicated session on how to pitch your idea in front of a jury. Learn to structure your talk, manage your 5 minutes, and make every word land. This is your last practice run before the real thing.
10:00 – 11:30
04
Pause Café
Coffee break. Recharge and get ready for the final stretch.
11:30 – 12:30
05
Pitch Rehearsal
One hour to rehearse your presentation, designate your speaker, and finalize your delivery. The IEEE committee is available for last-minute coaching.
12:30 – 13:30
06
Pitch Session
Each team presents to the jury — 5 minutes to pitch, followed by jury Q&A. All teams pitch one after another. Jury scores independently after each presentation.
13:30 – 15:00
07
Closing Ceremony & Winner Announcement
Jury feedback, winner announcement, and certificate distribution. All teams are recognized. The portal closes.
